# Mass Shootings in the United States: Trends & Associated Factors

> A data-driven analysis of mass shootings in the U.S., integrating multiple socio-economic datasets to examine long-term trends and associated factors.

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes mass shooting incidents in the United States from 1966 to 2021 by combining multiple data sources to explore demographic, temporal, and socio-economic patterns. The analysis focuses on understanding trends over time and identifying factors potentially associated with mass shooting incidents.

## 🎯 Objectives
- Examine long-term trends in mass shooting incidents
- Identify seasonal and temporal patterns
- Identify significant factors associated with shootings 
- EProviding recommendations to reduce mass shootings  

## 📊 Data Sources
The analysis combines multiple datasets, including:
- U.S. Mass Shootings Dataset (Kaggle)
- Gun Ownership Data (Violence Policy Center)
- Poverty Rate Data (U.S.)
- Income Inequality Data (Gini Index, U.S. Census Bureau)
- IMDB media datasets related to crime and serial killer content

## ⚙️ Methodology
- Data cleaning and preprocessing across multiple sources
- Integration of datasets into case-level and yearly aggregated datasets
- Exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- TIme series Trend and pattern analysis
- Socio-economic association analysis

## 📈 Key Findings
- Mass shooting incidents show an increasing trend over time
- Temporal and seasonal patterns are observable
- Associations were identified with income inequality, poverty rates, and media exposure indicators
- Demographic patterns indicate concentration in specific groups
